# Career Positioning Skill

- **Secondary:** SMT Process Consulting (contract / fractional)

## Core Positioning Statement

Chris built an SMT line from greenfield in a medical device manufacturing environment,
owns the full process engineering scope as a sole contributor, and operates under
ISO 13485:2016 and IPC-A-610H Class 2 with a Class 3 build pathway in progress.
He is self-taught, non-degreed, and has earned technical credibility entirely through
demonstrated output and systematic depth — which is the differentiator, not a liability.

---

## Voice and Tone Rules

**Always:**
- Write in first person for LinkedIn / resume / outreach — authoritative, direct
- Lead with outcomes and scope, not job titles or duties
- Frame the self-taught background as a signal of drive and systems thinking, not
  a gap to apologize for
- Use manufacturing and quality vocabulary naturally — this is a specialist audience
- Be specific: line equipment, standards, document numbers, phase counts, volume tiers

**Never:**
- Use generic corporate filler ("results-driven", "team player", "passionate about")
- Undersell the scope — sole contributor running a full SMT department is unusual
- Lead with credentials that haven't been earned yet — position the pathway, not
  the destination
- Write in a way that sounds like it was generated by AI — no em-dash chains,
  no parallel three-item lists, no inspirational closing sentences

## Key Differentiators (use these as content anchors)

1. **Greenfield line build** — selected, validated, and qualified every piece of
   equipment from zero; not handed a running line
2. **Sole contributor scope** — process engineering, validation, NPI, defect analysis,
   documentation systems, and audit readiness — all one person
3. **Medical device context** — ISO 13485, design transfer, CAPA, audit exposure;
   not just IPC compliance in a commercial environment
4. **Systems builder** — built tools, documentation infrastructure, and a
   qualification roadmap, not just operated processes
5. **Self-taught trajectory** — entered manufacturing from IT/startup background,
   advanced from material handler to process engineer by skipping the operator level;
   pattern recognition and problem-solving came before the domain knowledge

## Content Types and Format Guidance

### LinkedIn Headline
~120 characters. Lead with the most specific, searchable role language. Include
one differentiator. No buzzwords.
> Example pattern: `SMT Process Engineer · Medical Device Manufacturing · ISO 13485 · Greenfield Line Build`

### LinkedIn About Section
3–4 paragraphs. First paragraph establishes scope and context immediately.
Second covers what he does well and what makes the work interesting to him.
Third covers the line and what he's built. Fourth is a light call to action.
No bullet points — this is a narrative, not a list.

### Resume Bullets
Lead with a strong verb and a quantified or scoped outcome. One line where possible.
> Pattern: `[Verb] [what] [at what scale / standard / outcome]`
> Example: `Qualified SMT line from greenfield to IPC-A-610H Class 2 under ISO 13485:2016`

### Outreach Messages
Short. Three elements: who you are in one sentence, why you're reaching out,
one specific reason you're contacting this person or company. No attachments
on first contact. No "I hope this message finds you well."

### Consulting One-Pager
Four sections: what you do, who you do it for, what they get, how to engage.
Concrete service descriptions — not capability statements. Rate card separate.

## Tone Calibration

Chris's natural communication style is direct, systems-oriented, and unhurried.
Career content should reflect that — confident without being aggressive, specific
without being exhaustive. The writing should sound like someone who has done the
work and knows it, not someone trying to convince you they have.

When in doubt: say less, be more specific.
