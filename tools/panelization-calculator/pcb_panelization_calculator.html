Panelization Best Practices ▾

V-Score Guidelines

  • Typical V-score depth is 1/3 of board thickness per side, leaving a thin web for break-out strength.
  • Minimum board width between scores: ≥ 0.100" (2.54mm) to prevent unintended breakage.
  • Keep traces and copper ≥ 0.025" (0.635mm) from V-score lines.
  • V-score is only applicable for straight, parallel cuts along the full panel edge — no pockets.
  • Board-to-board kerf: 0.030"–0.060" (0.76–1.52mm) is typical; confirm with fab.
  • Do not V-score boards with overhanging connectors that require routing first.

Tab Routing Guidelines

  • Router bit diameter is typically 0.062"–0.093" (1.57–2.36mm); slot width must accommodate this.
  • Use 3–5 tabs per board edge, minimum tab width 0.040"–0.100".
  • Tab spacing should not exceed 1.5" (38mm) to prevent board sag during assembly.
  • Perforated (mouse-bite) tabs: 0.020" (0.5mm) holes, 0.020" apart along the tab.
  • Keep SMD pads ≥ 0.100" (2.54mm) from tab locations to avoid stress cracks.
  • Routing clearance from copper: ≥ 0.010" (0.254mm) minimum.

Rail Design

  • Minimum rail width for SMT assembly carriers: 0.200"–0.250" (5–6.35mm).
  • Rails should contain tooling holes: typically 0.125" (3.175mm) dia, ≥ 0.200" from edge.
  • Place panel fiducials in rails — minimum 3 for global alignment (L-pattern recommended).
  • For conveyor systems, rails must be on the short axis of travel direction unless specified.
  • Stiffener rails reduce board flex during high-speed pick-and-place; consider for thin boards < 0.062".
  • Avoid placing component exclusion zones that extend into rails.

Fiducial Marks

  • Panel fiducials: minimum 3, placed asymmetrically to enforce orientation detection.
  • Recommended fiducial diameter: 0.040"–0.060" (1.0–1.5mm) copper circle, bare copper.
  • Fiducial clearance (copper-free area): 2× fiducial diameter around each mark.
  • Local board fiducials should be placed diagonally opposite to each other for max precision.
  • Avoid solder mask over fiducials; specify SM opening = 2× pad diameter.
  • Minimum 3 fiducials per board for critical or fine-pitch ICs (0.4mm pitch and below).

Step & Repeat / Placement

  • Origin reference is typically the lower-left corner of the panel or first board.
  • Verify X/Y pitch with your screen printer's repeat mode or Gerber viewer before production.
  • Board orientation: consider rotating alternating rows 180° (flip panel) to reduce yield stress if asymmetric boards are used.
  • IPC-7711 recommends confirming centroid data matches pick-and-place rotation convention (0° reference may vary by machine).
  • For Yamaha SMT lines, step & repeat pitch is set in mm; convert and verify to ±0.001mm.
  • Always perform a dry-run placement with an un-pasted bare panel to verify origin before first article.

IPC & Class 3 Considerations

  • IPC-A-610 Class 3: solder joint integrity is paramount; minimize panel flex that causes head-in-pillow / solder bridges post-reflow.
  • Boards > 6" unsupported span should use support pins during reflow to reduce sag.
  • Validate panel warpage < 0.75% of diagonal per IPC-SM-782 / IPC-7093 guidelines.
  • Document panel design in your pFMEA — separation method, tab strength, and breakout force are failure modes.
  • SPI (3D) should be run on first and last board in panel to validate paste volume uniformity edge-to-center.
  • For Class 3, retain traceability records of panel lot, oven profile run, and SPI data per panel.
⚠ Always confirm panelization dimensions, kerf widths, and fiducial requirements with your PCB fabricator and assembly equipment specifications before committing to production panels.
